## Selected work

### Drive Mad · Level Design & Prototyping

<a href="https://poki.com/en/g/drive-mad">
  <img src="assets/Drive_Mad_Level_Prototype_1.png" alt="A Drive Mad level prototype with a monster truck, curved track, and loop" width="100%">
</a>

I worked with **Fancade for nearly two years**, developing game prototypes and extensive level content. For *Drive Mad*, I designed and iterated approximately **200 levels for later content**, shaping difficulty, pacing, mechanical variation, and progression around the game's two-button physics.

The result is a useful example of how I like to work: start with a compact ruleset, explore its edge cases, and turn those discoveries into readable challenges. The public game now spans 250 levels and, according to Fancade's public listing, has been played more than 300 million times.

**Contribution:** later level content and prototyping · **Original game:** Martin Magni / Fancade

[Play Drive Mad →](https://poki.com/en/g/drive-mad)

## How I work

I am most useful where a project needs to move from **idea to playable truth**. I prototype the riskiest interaction early, build the supporting systems, and iterate until the mechanic communicates itself.

- Rapid gameplay prototypes and mechanic exploration
- Physics-based construction and interaction systems
- Level design, difficulty curves, and content iteration
- Mobile, web, and social-platform experiences
- Turning broad game ideas into bounded development plans

## Currently exploring

- **Project Forge** — a visual planning environment for turning complex ideas into practical roadmaps and working effectively with AI development agents.
- **Deterministic contraptions** — a Godot and C# experiment with rods, wheels, motors, obstacles, and reproducible physics-driven construction.
